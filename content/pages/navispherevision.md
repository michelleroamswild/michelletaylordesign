# Research Finding #1
finding1_label: "Research Finding #1"
finding1_title: "Shipments Appear Missing"
finding1_quote: "I don't use Vision because my shipments don't show up when I search for them."
finding1_quoteAttribution: "-- Navisphere Vision user research participant"
finding1_cards:
  - icon: "MapTrifold"
    title: "Map vs. list view confusion"
    description: "The split between map and list views created confusion. Users couldn't understand why shipments visible on the map weren't appearing in their list."
  - icon: "ListBullets"
    title: "List view button hidden by filters"
    description: "The toggle to switch to list-only view was buried beneath active filters, making it nearly invisible to most users during their daily workflows."
  - icon: "Package"
    title: "Map not needed for daily tasks"
    description: "Most users didn't need the map for their daily shipment management tasks. They wanted a simple, scannable list of their shipments with status and ETA information."

# Research Finding #2
finding2_label: "Research Finding #2"
finding2_title: "Shipment Cards & Detail Pages"
finding2_cards:
  - title: "Hard to navigate orders on multiple loads"
    description: "Users managing orders spread across several loads struggled to track individual shipments within nested, collapsed card structures."
  - title: "Tracking buried at bottom"
    description: "The most critical information -- live tracking data -- was pushed to the bottom of detail pages, requiring excessive scrolling."
  - title: "No data hierarchy"
    description: "All information was displayed with equal visual weight, making it impossible to quickly scan for the most urgent data points."
  - title: "Overcrowded layout"
    description: "Detail pages tried to show everything at once, creating dense, overwhelming screens that slowed decision-making."

# Solution #2
solution2_label: "Solution for Problem #2"
solution2_title: "Redesigned Shipment Cards & Tracking"
solution2_description: "We restructured shipment detail pages to surface the most critical information first and provide clear navigation for complex, multi-load orders."
solution2_items:
  - icon: "Package"
    title: "Expandable cards for multiple loads"
    description: "Orders with multiple loads now use expandable card structures, letting users drill into individual load details without losing context of the full order."
  - icon: "NavigationArrow"
    title: "Separate tracking tab"
    description: "Live tracking information was elevated to its own dedicated tab, making it immediately accessible instead of buried at the bottom of the page."
  - icon: "CalendarDots"
    title: "Multiple date fields"
    description: "Added distinct date fields for scheduled pickup, estimated delivery, and actual delivery -- giving users a complete timeline at a glance."
  - icon: "Eye"
    title: "Clarified ETA sources"
    description: "ETA estimates now clearly indicate their data source (carrier, GPS, predictive model) so users can gauge confidence in delivery projections."
